
# AAA Creature Design: The Resonance Weaver (Evolution of The Threadling)

## 1. Core Concept
The Resonance Weaver is a 20-meter-tall predatory entity that acts as the "immune system" of the REDROOM. It is blind, sound-sensitive, and moves via inverted suspension.

## 2. Silhouette & Anatomy
- **The Hub:** Instead of a head, it has a cluster of three translucent resonance bladders (pale, flayed muscle textures).
- **The Needles:** 14 asymmetrical, multi-jointed limbs that function like weaver's needles. Some limbs are used for locomotion (anchoring into flesh walls), while others hang loosely to brush against surfaces for vibrations.
- **The Form:** In the cramped 6m rooms, it appears "folded." Limbs are constantly shifting, twitching, and re-arranging.

## 3. Sensory Organs: Acoustic Resonance
- **Resonance sacs:** These vibrate visibly to the player's heartbeat. If the sac turns bright red, the creature has locked onto the sound source.
- **No Eyes:** The face is a flat, bone-like surface with porous holes for sound intake.

## 4. Locomotion: Threaded Suspension
The Weaver does not "walk." It threads its limbs into the organic architecture of the maze. It can exist on the ceiling, walls, or floor simultaneously. 

## 5. Movement Studies
- **Idle:** Limbs twitch slowly; resonance sacs pulsate rhythmically.
- **Detection:** The entire creature freezes. The resonance sacs swell and turn a deep crimson.
- **Hunt:** The creature "unfolds," its 20m span visible as it twitches across the ceiling at high speed toward the player.

## 6. Variant: The Ceiling Dweller
The version encountered in the REDROOM is primarily a ceiling-dweller, forcing the player to walk *under* its hanging sensory tendrils.
